Course Overview

Calculus provides the mathematical tools for understanding rates of change, optimization, and accumulation. In software engineering, these concepts are crucial for algorithm analysis, machine learning optimization, numerical methods, and understanding computational complexity.

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Understand differentiation and its applications in optimization
  • Learn integration techniques and the Fundamental Theorem
  • Apply multivariable calculus to higher-dimensional problems
  • Connect calculus concepts to software engineering applications

Practice Problems

Limits

Basic: Limit Definition

Define the concept of a limit and explain its purpose in calculus.

Intermediate: Computing Limits

Apply the concept to compute \(\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^2 - 4}{x - 2}\).

Advanced: Limit Properties

Analyze and prove properties related to limits of functions.

Continuity

Basic: Continuity Definition

Define continuity and explain why it's important in mathematics.

Intermediate: Continuity Testing

Apply continuity concepts to analyze \(f(x) = \frac{x^2 - 1}{x - 1}\).

Advanced: Continuity Proofs

Prove theorems about continuous functions on intervals.

Differentiation

Basic: Derivative Definition

Define the derivative and explain its geometric interpretation.

Intermediate: Differentiation Rules

Apply product rule, quotient rule, and chain rule to compute derivatives.

Advanced: Implicit Differentiation

Use implicit differentiation to find \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) for complex equations.

Integration

Basic: Antiderivatives

Find antiderivatives of basic functions and understand the integration process.

Intermediate: Definite Integrals

Compute definite integrals and understand their geometric interpretation.

Advanced: Integration Techniques

Apply substitution, integration by parts, and trigonometric substitution.

Multivariable Calculus

Basic: Partial Derivatives

Compute partial derivatives of multivariable functions.

Intermediate: Gradient Vectors

Find gradient vectors and understand their geometric meaning.

Advanced: Lagrange Multipliers

Apply constrained optimization using Lagrange multipliers.
