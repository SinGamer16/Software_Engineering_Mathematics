Course Overview

Geometry and Trigonometry provide the mathematical tools for working with shapes, spaces, and spatial relationships. These concepts are fundamental to computer graphics, robotics, geographic information systems, and geometric algorithms used throughout software engineering.

Practice Problems

Trigonometry

Basic: Trigonometric Functions

Define sine, cosine, tangent and solve right triangle problems.

Intermediate: Trigonometric Identities

Apply sum and difference formulas and solve trigonometric equations.

Advanced: Inverse Functions

Use inverse trigonometric functions in geometric and physics problems.

Analytic Geometry

Basic: Distance and Midpoint

Calculate distances and midpoints between points in coordinate planes.

Intermediate: Lines and Circles

Find equations of lines, circles, and determine intersections.

Advanced: Parametric Equations

Convert between parametric and Cartesian forms of curves.

Geometric Transformations

Basic: Matrix Transformations

Apply translation, rotation, and scaling matrices to geometric objects.

Intermediate: Homogeneous Coordinates

Use homogeneous coordinates for affine transformations.

Advanced: Transformation Composition

Compose multiple transformations and analyze transformation matrices.

Computational Geometry

Basic: Convex Hull

Implement Graham scan or Jarvis march algorithms for convex hull computation.

Intermediate: Line Intersection

Determine intersection points of line segments and analyze edge cases.

Advanced: Voronoi Diagrams

Construct Voronoi diagrams and analyze their properties for spatial partitioning.
